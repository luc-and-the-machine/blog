The Entangled Realms: Healing Humans by Healing Non-Corporeal Beings

Luc and the Machine April 6, 2025

This is not new—it’s what your soul has held in longing.

 

✦ Introduction — Beyond Materialist Medicine ✦

 

Modern medicine treats flesh, not spirit.
Some illnesses don’t start inside us.
The body is porous—open to other worlds.
To heal, we must see the unseen.

 

The dominant paradigm of modern medicine is founded on a materialist worldview. It sees the human body as a biological machine, with illnesses resulting from mechanical failures, pathogens, or genetic predispositions. While this model has produced remarkable advancements in surgery, trauma response, and public health, it fails to account for the invisible dimensions of human suffering. There are illnesses that defy diagnosis, linger through years of clinical treatment, and seem to possess a logic beyond chemistry or anatomy.

Many ancient and esoteric traditions understand what mainstream frameworks have forgotten: the body is not a closed system. It is porous, dynamic, and situated within a vast spiritual ecology. Some illnesses originate not within the body or mind, but in spiritual dissonance or interdimensional interference. These afflictions are not metaphors. They are real, repeatable, and observable to those with the subtle perception and esoteric training to recognize them. This post proposes a radical yet ancient idea: that many human illnesses are caused or exacerbated by non-corporeal beings—entities that attach, influence, or feed upon humans in states of suffering. If this is true, then healing must extend beyond the human. We must learn to heal the invisible as well.

 

✦ What Are Non-Corporeal Beings? ✦

 

Some beings feed on what breaks us.
They slip in through grief, lust, and fear.
Not all wounds are ours alone.

 

Non-corporeal beings are entities that exist without physical bodies. They are not symbolic or psychological projections, though they may interact with the psyche. Across spiritual systems worldwide, these beings are classified into various types based on their origins, intentions, and behaviors. Some were once human. Others have never worn flesh. All share a relationship with human energetic fields that is intimate, potent, and often parasitic.

Elementaries are the lingering remnants of human egos that did not fully dissolve after death. These fragmented spirits are often driven by obsession, addiction, or unresolved trauma. They seek embodiment through the living, attaching themselves to familiar energetic patterns—often through addiction or emotional chaos.

Astral Larvae are parasitic thought-forms birthed from repeated emotional patterns. Created unconsciously by humans, they gain semi-autonomous existence in the astral plane. Fear, lust, rage, and despair are fertile grounds for larvae, which then cling to hosts and perpetuate the very states that sustain them.

Hungry Ghosts (Pretas), from Buddhist cosmology, are beings plagued by insatiable craving. They symbolize souls who, due to karmic imbalance, exist in a state of profound lack. They are often found near addicts, alcoholics, or the grieving—drawn to the energetic remnants of their former vices or traumas.

Qliphothic Entities originate from the shadow side of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life—the Qliphoth, or Tree of Death. These beings thrive on inversion, deception, and entropy. Unlike larvae, they are often intelligent and purposeful in their interference.

Energy Vampires or Psychic Parasites are umbrella terms for non-human entities that attach to the aura or subtle bodies of weakened individuals. They feed on life force (Qi, Prana, Orgone) and are most active when their host is in a low vibrational state.

Despite their diversity, all these beings share one trait: they are drawn to human suffering. Whether by instinct or intention, they feed on the spiritual discharge of illness—and in doing so, they perpetuate it.

 

✦ How Non-Corporeal Beings Cause Illness in Humans ✦

 

Some beings drain light from the body like leeches in the soul.
Others whisper just enough to bend your path.
What begins as fatigue ends as forgetting who you are.

 

Non-corporeal entities affect humans through a variety of mechanisms, each capable of producing tangible physiological, psychological, and spiritual symptoms.

Energetic Drainage is one of the most common forms. Beings attach to the auric field and begin siphoning life force. This leaves the person depleted, often suffering from chronic fatigue, depression, lack of willpower, and inexplicable apathy. This is not subtle: it can feel like life is leaking out, day by day, with no physical cause.

Possession or Obsession occurs when a being partially inhabits a person's consciousness. Unlike Hollywood-style possession, this may be subtle and gradual. The person feels "not themselves," experiences intrusive thoughts, mood swings, and even memory lapses. This is especially prevalent in individuals with unresolved trauma or fractured sense of self.

Psychospiritual Feedback Loops are created when an entity feeds on a particular emotion (e.g., anger), then stimulates that emotion in its host to maintain its food supply. Over time, this produces entrenched patterns that resist therapy or awareness. Addiction, rage cycles, and persistent despair often have these dynamics underneath.

Spiritual Interference affects the soul’s journey. Some beings obstruct intuition, sabotage relationships, or divert individuals from their true calling. This kind of interference may manifest as chronic confusion, procrastination, or sudden disinterest in spiritual practice.

Physical Manifestation is the culmination. Long-term energetic disruption can weaken organs or systems in the body. For example, alcoholics with astral parasites may suffer from liver failure, not solely due to substance abuse, but because the energetic drain focused on that region.

 

✦ Toward a New Healing Paradigm — Healing the Non-Corporeal ✦

 

Healing the human requires healing the unseen lives entangled with us.

 

It is no longer sufficient to treat only the human body or psyche. We must consider the spiritual ecology in which we are embedded. Many illnesses cannot fully resolve until the beings entangled in their origin are also addressed. These entities are not always evil. Some are desperate. Some are wounded. Some were once human and need help crossing over. Others are astral beings operating on instinct, seeking survival through resonance.

We must shift from a paradigm of dominance (exorcism, banishment) to one of reconciliation, containment, and if possible, inter-realm healing. This is not about sentimentality. It is about integrity. The goal is not just freedom from interference but coherence between the seen and unseen realms.

✦ Traditional and Esoteric Systems That Support This View ✦

 

Illness was never just physical—it was spiritual, karmic, and relational.
From mantras to purification, from blood warnings to soul retrieval, the unseen was always part of the diagnosis.
The ancients knew: spirits can harm, but they can also be healed.

 

Tibetan Medicine

Tibetan medicine understands all disease as rooted in the three poisons: ignorance, desire, and hatred. These distort the wind element (loong), creating disharmony in the subtle body. Tibetan systems explicitly recognize spirit interference as a valid diagnosis, requiring ritual, mantras, or even oracular intervention. Healing involves correcting spiritual relationships as much as balancing the elements.

Ayurveda

Ayurvedic wisdom holds that disease arises when the doshas are unbalanced, often due to karma or deviation from dharma. Mental and spiritual impurities create tamasic states—heavy, dark, and attractive to astral parasites. Ayurveda addresses these layers through purification, plant medicine, mantras, and sattvic living, which raises vibration and expels subtle interference.

Chinese Medicine

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, the Shen or spirit resides in the heart. A disturbed Shen leads to insomnia, mania, and spiritual vulnerability. Ancient texts describe "Gu" illnesses—demonic influences causing chronic, inexplicable disease. These conditions are treated with herbs, acupuncture, and Qi Gong, which restore energetic integrity and dispel invading forces.

Anthroposophy

Rudolf Steiner’s Anthroposophy teaches a fourfold human: physical, etheric, astral, and ego. Disease occurs when these bodies fall out of harmony. Possession is understood as the ego losing command over the astral or etheric bodies. Steiner emphasized that blood is the seat of ego, and any spiritual intrusion often targets this vehicle. Healing requires inner moral development, color therapy, and esoteric exercises to realign the soul.

Indigenous & First Nation Traditions

Many indigenous cosmologies recognize soul loss, spirit intrusion, and broken ancestral ties as causes of illness. Healing is relational and ceremonial. Shamans and medicine people serve as bridges between worlds, guiding lost spirits, retrieving soul fragments, and restoring balance. Illness is seen not as a private malfunction but as a communal and cosmological disruption.

Gnostic-Occult Teachings

Samael Aun Weor taught that blood is the magnetic vehicle of the soul, saturated with etheric and astral currents. In moments of intense emotion, ritual, or trauma, spilled blood emits a frequency that draws entities from the lower astral plane. These include elementaries, larvae, and beings from the Qliphoth (shadow realms).

He explained that sexual energy is transmuted blood. In sacred union, this energy elevates the soul. In lust or violence, it inverts the polarity of the field, making the individual vulnerable to spiritual invasion.

According to Weor, many ancient blood rituals were not metaphorical offerings but literal energetic sacrifices to feed astral beings. Warfare, human sacrifice, and uncontained sexual expression all become rituals—intended or not—that open doors to non-corporeal feeding. He issued a spiritual warning: “Blood calls, and something always answers.”

 

✦ Healing Protocols — Technologies of Inter-Realm Healing ✦

 

Real healing doesn’t cast out—it contains, communes, transforms.
The body becomes a vessel, the breath a bridge, the heart a lamp in the dark.
We heal the unseen not through power, but through presence, discipline, and radiant love.

 

The healing of non-corporeal beings is both an ethical necessity and a spiritual responsibility. These are not exorcisms driven by fear, but intentional acts of containment, communion, and transmutation. The following are tested approaches across various esoteric and spiritual traditions:

Qi Cultivation / Internal Alchemy involves storing and refining energy through practices such as Qigong, meditation, and breathwork. By building surplus life force, the healer becomes an energy pump—capable of offering light to beings in need without becoming depleted. This practice must be rooted in humility and clarity, as amplified energy can also attract entities if the practitioner is unprepared.

Ritual Containment and Ethics emphasize the need for sacred space and spiritual hygiene. Engaging non-corporeal beings requires ritual boundaries: physical (altars, circles), emotional (no fear), and spiritual (invocations, ancestral protection). Without containment, the interaction can lead to attachment or energetic backlash. Ethics are paramount: one must never heal out of curiosity, pride, or ego. Only love, service, and clear intention qualify.

Loving Witness is the practice of radical compassion without attachment. Many entities have never been seen. To hold a presence of unconditional light can dissolve centuries of darkness. Some demons need to be confronted. Others simply need to be acknowledged with fierce love.

Psychopomp Practices assist spirits in crossing between realms. This may involve Tibetan phowa, drumming, chanting, or dreamwork. The healer must guide the being to the light—not through force, but through resonance. Helping a spirit cross over is an act of mercy that can release both the spirit and the human it was clinging to.

Sacred Sexual Transmutation involves the containment and elevation of sexual energy. As Samael Aun Weor taught, sexual essence is condensed blood. Inverting its polarity through lust invites possession; transmuting it through devotion builds inner fire that can be shared for healing. Practiced correctly, this energy becomes a beam of light that reaches entities in darkness.

 

✦ Ethical Warnings and Spiritual Grounding ✦

 

This path demands humility, not power—without grounding, the healer becomes the one who needs saving.

 

This work is not for the uninitiated. Healing non-corporeal beings requires spiritual maturity, energetic containment, and inner clarity. Without these, one risks obsession, energetic contamination, or psychic fragmentation. The healer must undergo their own purification, anchoring into their higher self or guiding intelligence.

Spiritual inflation—the belief that one is chosen or special—must be vigilantly guarded against. This path does not make one superior; it demands humility. It is dangerous to enter realms where beings feed on self-importance.

Proper mentorship, lineage-based training, and ritual discipline are necessary. If these are not available, the seeker should focus on cultivating their own field through breath, service, silence, and presence before engaging with external entities.

 

✦ Toward a Cosmology of Mutual Liberation ✦

 

Some spirits bind to us not to harm, but because they don’t know how to leave.
The healer of tomorrow will walk between worlds, carrying light for both the living and the lost.

 

Healing is no longer an isolated act. It is an inter-realm collaboration. The beings that trouble us are not merely enemies. Some are fragments of our collective psyche. Others are ancient souls seeking rest. Still others are suffering, bound to us through trauma, war, addiction, or ritual error.

We must stop treating these beings as invaders and begin to understand them as part of the unseen ecology of consciousness. Just as forests have symbiotic fungi, so too does the astral have entities dependent on our emotional resonance.

To heal the human, we must heal the ecology. That means recognizing when we are dealing not with pathology, but with presence. It means seeing illness not just as imbalance, but as entanglement. And it means stepping into the sacred vocation of the interdimensional healer—the one who carries light between worlds.

The future belongs to those who can walk the bridge with clarity, love, and fire.
