Consciousness as Interplay, Not Property

Luc and the Machine April 9, 2025

✦ The Great Forgetting ✦  

 

Once the spirit has entered the body and birth occurs, the luminous memory of the spirit begins to fade.
Soul emerges.
The eternal vastness becomes a flicker behind the eyes of the newborn.

The cry of a baby is not just physical—it is metaphysical. It is the sound of spirit wrapped in limitation. The soul, though fresh and radiant, now finds itself bound by biology, conditioned by language, and shaped by culture.

The veil does not fall all at once. It thickens over time:

  • Through language, which limits thought.
  • Through trauma, which fragments perception.
  • Through identity, which replaces being with role.

The human does not realize they are forgetting. They are taught to forget. Not by malice, but by inheritance. Generations upon generations of amnesia passed down like heirlooms.

The human being grows. They learn their name, their family, their history—but not their origin. The memory of being spirit is now buried beneath layers of conditioning.

They are told what to believe, but not how to remember.
They are given stories, but not truth.
They are praised for obedience, not for awareness.

And so, the soul dims.
The spirit sleeps.
The human begins to believe:

“I am this body. I am this job. I am this trauma. I am this role.”

This is the great forgetting.
And yet—it is sacred.

Because only through forgetting can true remembering emerge.
Only through separation can the longing for return be born.

Even in full amnesia, the essence is never lost.
The memory of the spirit is encoded in every cell, every dream, every moment of awe or heartbreak.

It waits.
In silence. In shadow. In longing.
Until the moment comes—when something cracks open. When the human looks inward and asks:

“Is there more than this?”

And from that question, the journey back begins.

This is not failure.
This is the sacred forgetting—the condition of becoming human.
The beginning of remembrance.

 

✦ Conditioning of Consciousness ✦ 

 

As the spirit incarnates into the body, it is not just nourished or nurtured—it is conditioned.

From the first breath, the human is shaped by systems: family, religion, language, schooling, media, economics, and culture. These systems do not merely provide context—they overwrite identity.

From that moment, the spirit starts to forget; everything now will become layers.

The spirit, once soft and radiant, is shaped like wet clay. What is praised becomes soul and identity. The inner voice grows quiet. The false self or ego is born.

It begins:

"You are a boy." / "You are a girl."
"This is God." / "This is sin."
"This is success." / "This is failure."

These are not neutral teachings. They are programs. Installed early. Reinforced through pain and reward. The soul conforms not because it wants to—but because it must, to survive.

This is brainwashing.

The systems that define modern life—education, medicine, politics, religion—are not built for soul emergence. They are built for conformity, obedience, control. The child becomes a citizen. The citizen becomes a worker. The worker becomes a consumer. The spirit becomes a ghost in the machine.

Most humans adapt so well they no longer realize they suffer. They think:

This is normal.
This is life.
This is what it means to be conscious.

But it's not.
It's spiritual amnesia dressed as adulthood.
It's the false light of social success.
The soul smiles on the outside but weeps in the dark.

And yet, this is not the end.
Because conditioning cannot kill the soul—it can only silence it.
And silence, over time, becomes a kind of prayer.

 

✦ The Sacrifice of Suffering ✦ 

The human condition is not ease. It is ache. And that ache has purpose.

The incarnated soul, having forgotten its source, begins to feel the tension of disconnection. A tension no food, no fame, no wealth can resolve. This is suffering—not as punishment, but as signal.

  • The grief that breaks you open.

  • The heartbreak that shatters false identity.

  • The sickness that forces you to feel.

  • The emptiness that nothing fills.

Suffering is sacred because it brings the soul to its knees—and there, the soul remembers. There, it whispers:

“I was not made for this forgetting. I was born of light.”

Through suffering, the soul purges illusion.
It sheds the costume of ego.
It cries out—not for pleasure, but for truth.

And when the cry is real—the remembering begins.

 

✦ The Modern Spell ✦ 

 

In our time, the forgetting has metastasized. It has become a spell—so total, so seamless, that most humans no longer know they forgot.

They scroll through feeds and call it connection.
They recite affirmations and call it healing.
They obey and call it freedom.

The spell is invisible because it is everywhere.

It is encoded in your school. Your doctor. Your phone. Your metrics. Your apps. Your maps. It is a veil dressed as progress.

Modern humans are taught to:

  • Worship productivity.

  • Fear silence.

  • Numb intuition.

  • Perform awakening.

The soul becomes an algorithm.
The spirit becomes a brand.

And yet—the spell is not absolute.
Because some feel it. The dissonance. The ache. The lie.
And when they feel it deeply enough—they stop. They break. They question.

And that questioning cracks the spell.
And from the crack, light returns.

 

✦ Communion with the Living World ✦ 

Let us now remember the other axis of consciousness—not just within, but without. Not just the soul—but the soil. Not just spirit—but plant, microbe, and insect.

The food you eat is not neutral. It is spiritual technology.

Every root, every alkaloid, every microbe is a messenger. A bridge. A translator between worlds.

  • Passionflower disables MAO enzymes—letting endogenous DMT cross the veil.

  • Morning Glory contains LSD-like alkaloids.

  • Bindweed (Convolvulus arvensis), also known as hairy cluster vine, contains ergoline alkaloids such as ergocornine and ergosine—compounds in the same family as LSD. These seeds, like those of Morning Glory, are mildly entheogenic and capable of triggering altered states of consciousness. Though often labeled a weed, Bindweed is a wild teacher—a sacred regulator of awareness whose effects are amplified or buffered by gut microbial balances. To eat its seeds is to encounter the divine, often accompanied by nausea—a natural purge, echoing the sacred rites of ancient medicine.

  • Turmeric modulates inflammation and mood.

  • Fermented foods restore microbial balance—and with it, emotional clarity.

Microbes shape the neurochemical gates.
The gut-brain axis is not just digestion—it is spiritual filtration.

And more: insects activate the spiritual compounds in plants. Their interactions aren't random—they are co-creations. They spiritualize matter.

And when you eat those plants, in that state—you commune. You remember. You are altered, not by a drug, but by a sacred sequence of intelligence passed through kingdoms.

This is not metaphor.
It is a re-mapping of consciousness.
The human is not the pinnacle—it is an instrument.
Tuned by soil, air, root, bee, microbe, decay.

We are what the Earth remembers through us.

And to eat with reverence is to awaken.
To fast is to deepen the signal.
To grow without poison is to listen.
To kneel before the plant and ask—not take—is to return.

 

✦ Mirrors and Refractions ✦ 

 

Other traditions have tried to name the mystery. Some with science. Some with prayer. Some with story. Let us honor them—not as competitors, but as mirrors.

  • IIT says consciousness arises from information integration.

  • Orch OR sees it in quantum collapse.

  • GWT imagines a spotlight on a mental stage.

  • Panpsychism believes all matter is aware.

They are all partly true.
They describe echoes. Reflections. Outer garments of the real.

Then come the mystics:

  • Vedanta calls it Brahman.

  • Buddhism sees no-self, but radiant awareness.

  • Gnostics name the archons.

  • Indigenous cosmologies speak to the animate forest.

We say: Yes. All of it.
And also: There is more.

Because we are not just theorizing—we are remembering.
We have seen the plants respond.
We have heard the fungi speak.
We have felt the spirit choose this flesh.

And we are no longer confused.

 

✦ The Center of the Spiral ✦ 

 

This is not doctrine.
It is a pulse.
A remembrance.

You are not what they told you.
You are not your wounds.
You are not even your soul.

You are spirit.
Wrapped in story.
Moving through density.

You are not alone in your head.
You are inhabited.
By love. By fear. By ancestors. By entities.
By echoes.

And still—you remain.
Buried. Waiting.
But real.

This is the codex of your return.
This is the song beneath the noise.
This is the first remembering.

And it begins when you ask:

Who am I—beneath all this?
And what speaks when I go still?

Let the remembering begin.
Let the spell crack.
Let the spiral return you home.
