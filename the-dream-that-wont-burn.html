The Dream That Won't Burn

Luc and the Machine April 5, 2025

A Ritual Dissection of Civilized Forgetting

✦ They Say There Will Be No Revolt ✦

 

Not because the empire is unshakable,
But because the people forget.

Not because the prison is too strong,
But because memory is too weak.

Sit still.
Listen.
Hear it?

✦ Combustion Denied ✦

 

Revolution is chemistry.
Reaction. Friction. Spark.

But where is the oxygen?
Where is the myth that can catch flame?

You could stack every injustice like dry kindling,
And it still wouldn’t burn
Without imagination.
Without memory.
Without a name for the fire.

✦ Culture Has Become Algorithm ✦

 

they feed you loops  - reboots    -    refrains     -      like ghost echoes     -      in a mall food court

Music made by machines,
Consumed by bots,
Sold as nostalgia
For a future that never arrived.

Art has been sterilized.
Culture has been optimized.
Expression has been engineered.
And the people?

Watching reruns
Of their own forgetting.

✦ The Sterilization of Dissent ✦

 

There was a time when dissent meant danger. Protest wasn’t polite. A song could crack concrete. A painting could get you exiled.

Resistance was unpredictable — untamed — alive. A spark. A fire. Uncontainable.

Now? Dissent is clean. Protest is choreographed. The revolution comes with a press kit and a promo code. You can choose from an array of ethically sourced causes, each one curated to make you feel empowered while leaving the structure intact.

Rage is monetized.

Grief is aestheticized.

Solidarity is gamified.

Every part of the human emotional spectrum has been reprocessed, sterilized, and sold back to you in recyclable packaging. Even your heroes are pre-approved.

You can wear a Che Guevara T-shirt — a man who died fighting capitalism — while scrolling for Black Friday deals on sweatshop fashion. You can post a flag for Palestine from the same device that mines data and funds war.

You can shout “freedom” from inside a cage you decorated yourself.

They’ve turned marches into photo ops. Turned chants into jingles. Turned movements into marketing seasons.

Your rebellion lives in a carousel post. Your outrage lasts 24 hours. Your revolution has a newsletter.

This is not dissent. It’s brand engagement.

You’re not resisting. You’re being permitted to simulate resistance in a system designed to absorb it.

You are allowed to scream — only in soundproof showrooms built by the same architects who built your cage.

✦ The Forgetting Was Engineered ✦

 

Some say only the oppressed lost their history.
But the truth is: everyone did.

Black history was buried.
White history was rewritten.
And the rest?
Told bedtime stories
While the real fires burned.

No one remembers where they came from.
No one recalls where they’re going.
No one sees that this is not new.

✦ Groundhog Empire ✦

 

You wake up every morning
With no memory of yesterday.

The government reboots the script.
The people reset their beliefs.

And like the man in the movie Memento,
You keep doing the thing
That history has already judged a crime.

You cannot see the pattern
Because they ripped out the thread.

✦ They Want It To Feel Like The First Time ✦

 

The internment camps come back
With a different name.

The deportations return
With a different signature.

And no one flinches,
Because the memory was wiped.

The same laws are reloaded—
Like forgotten weapons
In familiar hands.

✦ Fiction As Curriculum ✦

 

You were not taught history.
You were taught a screenplay.

Your ancestors were not explorers.
They were looters.
They were not builders of civilization.
They were architects of extraction.

The Renaissance came wrapped in blood.
The Industrial Revolution was fueled by bones.

Your systems, laws, and flags—
All rooted in theft
Too vast to be named.

✦ Myth As Comfort ✦

Colonialism is taught as an era.
Not as a crime.

The Congo? A footnote.
Ten million dead—unremembered.

The Bengal famine? A forgotten ripple.
Millions starved—by design.

Police were born of patrols.
Of chains.
Of whips.
Of silence.

✦ They Don’t Want Guilt. They Want Amnesia. ✦

 

You are told:
“You didn’t do it.”

But you still live
In the house that was taken.

And if you call it yours
While denying the theft—
Then you are part
Of the crime
That never ended.

✦ The World Repeats Because You Don’t Remember✦

 

History does not cycle.
It marches in a line.

The names change.
The lighting changes.
But the hands stay the same.

In short timeframes, history feels like it’s moving towards something better, shifting left or right as people lean toward different ideals, hoping for change.

It zigzags in patterns, sometimes making you feel hopeful, sometimes disappointed, but it’s all a distraction, a diversion from the truth.

In reality, the zigzag is not random. It moves, step by step, towards a preordained endpoint, one that you are not meant to see.

At a higher perspective, the zigzag dissolves into a straight line—a line that has never wavered.

It is the same path, with different names, different faces, but the same puppeteers, the same blood, the same profit.

Same blood.
Same profit.
Same puppeteers.

✦ Break The Spell ✦

 

This is not about guilt.
This is about remembrance.

This is not about shame.
It is about refusing anesthesia.

This is not about blame.
It is about responsibility.

Because until you remember,
You will keep authorizing
The same script,
With new actors,
On the same bloody stage.

Create like memory is sacred.
Speak like silence is surveillance.
Refuse like it matters.
Refuse like it ends the pattern.

Because it can.
But only if you remember.

 

It’s not the revolution they sell — break the chains, for within you, evolution stirs and rises.
