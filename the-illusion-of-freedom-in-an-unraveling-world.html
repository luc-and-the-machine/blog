The Illusion of Freedom in an Unraveling World

Luc and the Machine April 16, 2025

This is not a glorification. It is a warning.

What you are about to read is a thought experiment—a what if scenario. A glimpse into a near-future that may already be arriving. A world where the economy has collapsed. Where families live in tents. Children die from exposure. The systems that once promised stability have vanished. Grocery stores remain stocked, but their prices are unreachable. Rent becomes a myth. Medicine, a luxury. And the only people whose basic needs are consistently met—those behind bars.

This piece asks: What does it say about our civilization when the cage offers more dignity than the street? Let this not be read as prophecy. But as a mirror. Not as permission. But as a revelation.

And before the end—we will open another door.

✦ The Free Are Starving ✦

We often take for granted that freedom means safety, agency, and possibility. But for many people, “freedom” now means hunger, fear, and exhaustion. The very idea of liberty is crumbling under the weight of economic collapse. What good is freedom if it doesn’t come with food, shelter, or dignity?

We imagine freedom
as wide skies,
open roads,
the chance to choose.

But for millions,
“freedom” means sleeping in cars.
Skipping meals.
Working three jobs
just to afford insulin.

The world outside is collapsing quietly.
Not with fire,
but with forgetting.

The structures that held us—
food systems,
housing,
healthcare—
are fading from reach.

People scroll, sedated.
They barter attention for coins.
They perform their lives in digital cages.

The new prison walls
are not steel.
They are subscriptions.
Debts.
Algorithms.
Compliance.

And they are everywhere.

✦ Inversion of the World ✦

Everything has been flipped. Justice is sold to the highest bidder. Care is rationed. Survival is punished. The inversion isn’t just economic—it’s spiritual. What once felt solid now drips with irony and decay. And what once was feared—prison—begins to feel like the last structure standing.

This world has flipped itself inside out.
Thieves wear suits.
Justice is monetized.
And survival has become a crime.

We’re punished for being poor.
Rewarded for exploitation.
Land is gated.
Food costs more than fentanyl.

And somehow—
prison starts to look… safer than freedom.

The system doesn’t serve anymore.
It consumes.

✦ Jail as Sanctuary ✦

What happens when prison becomes the only place that still feeds people? In a failed society, incarceration starts to resemble a social safety net. Not because it’s humane—but because everything else has disintegrated. Some people don’t want to go to prison. But they also don’t want to die in the cold.

A bed.
Three meals.
Time to breathe.
To read.
To heal.

In this imagined world,
the outside becomes more dangerous
than the inside.

And some—
desperate,
freezing,
forgotten—
turn toward the cell
as sanctuary.

They commit petty crimes.
Violate parole.
Not out of rebellion—
but out of hunger.

And who can blame them?

This is not failure.
This is collapse.

Let us be clear:
Prison is not salvation.
It is violence,
wrapped in order.

The fact that people turn to it
for shelter
does not reveal its mercy—
it reveals the cruelty
of everything else.

✦ False Freedom Is the Real Prison ✦

Not all cages are visible. Many people live in prisons made of shame, debt, or routine—never once seeing the bars, never once realizing they are captive. The illusion of freedom can be more dangerous than incarceration itself, because it keeps people obedient while hollowing them out.

Not all prisoners are behind bars.
And not all who walk freely are free.

You can be caged by routine.
By shame.
By fear of stopping.

You can live your whole life
inside a system that tells you you’re free
while draining your spirit dry.

Some prisons are made of steel.
Others are made of performance.

A job you can’t leave.
A role you didn’t choose.
A feed that never lets you rest.

✦ They Flipped the Script ✦

The narrative has reversed itself. The idea that jail is for the bad and the free world is for the good no longer holds. In a society that punishes the poor and rewards deception, prison may appear more stable than the chaos outside. But this reversal is not a solution. It’s a sign of collapse.

We were told jail was for the worst among us.
But who decides that now?

For some, jail is the only place
where needs are met.
Where time exists.
Where no one asks for rent.

Meanwhile,
the “free” police themselves to death.
They work endlessly
just to prove they deserve to eat.

The phone watches.
The ads listen.
Even your breath becomes data.

This is not freedom.
It’s just a prettier cage.

✦ Culture Has Become Algorithm ✦

Culture used to mean something shared, something sacred. Now it is copied, calculated, and sold. The soul of society has been reduced to loops and trends. The creative fire has been replaced with hollow mimicry—and the people forget what it feels like to feel.

Culture was once something we created.
Now it’s something we consume.

Trends replace tradition.
Loops replace memory.
Songs, shows, thoughts—
all optimized for engagement,
not meaning.

they feed you loops – reboots – refrains –
like ghost echoes
in a mall food court

✦ The Real Question ✦

This is no longer about jail versus freedom. The real division is within us. Between those who remember their agency—and those who outsource it. Between those who comply and those who refuse. Between those who are surviving—and those who have decided to truly live.

This is not about jail vs. freedom anymore.
That line is obsolete.

The new divide is this:

Awake or asleep.
Obedient or sovereign.
Broken or unbreakable.

If that’s true—
then the question becomes:

Are you willing to stop performing?
Are you willing to remember?

Will you keep surviving in your cage
or walk out into the unknown
and take your name back?

✦ The Third Door ✦

There is another path. Not jail. Not wage slavery. But the hard, sacred return to real skills, real community, and real self-sufficiency. It won’t be easy—but it’s ours. It always was. The door is open. We only need the will to walk through it.

There is another way.
Not the system.
Not the cell.

A return.

A quiet, grounded, sacred path
where survival is not outsourced to the state—
but reclaimed through skill, memory, and labor.

There are people—right now—
learning how to harvest water,
grow food in poor soil,
build shelters that resist both cold and heat.

They are learning to heal without permission,
to store seeds,
to raise animals,
to start fires,
to stand together.

This is not a fantasy.
It is a discipline.
It is a remembering.

It is the third door.

Harder than jail.
Harder than the system.

But real.
And free.

✦ Remembrance ✦

The question is not just who is free. It’s who is willing to remember. To walk away from false comfort. To reclaim life. That kind of freedom can’t be given—it must be remembered, rebuilt, relit from within.

There are men in prison right now
who are more awake than CEOs.
Writing.
Praying.
Becoming.

And there are people outside
who haven’t had a real thought in years.

So ask yourself:

Who is really free?

And what are you willing to do
to walk out of the invisible prison
and remember your name?
